Himavant, who was returning from his morning walk, reached the same spot without fail. The day would begin with a morning stroll; he would have come after a bath. "Dear Prarthana, look, the directions of both our lives have changed. Who would have thought that I would spend my days like this with a friend at this turn of life? Himavant is such a wonderful human being. Ever since I made the decision to marry, he has become a little more silent than before. After that decision, Himavant's mysterious behaviors sometimes haunt me, becoming incomprehensible. He uses me as an excuse to step out of the house. On some nights, the tranquility in his eyes, as if he has returned from some meditation, is accompanied by a sense of anxiety. Does he fight with your memories? I don't know. Does he love me? I am not certain. Have I started loving him? I don't know. But I feel comfortable with him. You are not the kind of small-minded person who would think I have occupied your place, are you? I should imagine your place as my own; if not in this house, then in this world. How are your days, Debu and Hyderabad? I hope they are lovely days. Let us meet as soon as the classes begin. Perhaps you will see Himavant with me. By the way, there is a phone at Himu’s house; call me when you are free. Convey my regards to Debu."

Urmila had written a small letter, enclosed the number of Himu’s house, and dropped it into the mailbox. While Urmila was writing the letter in the inland cover, she noticed Zabeen pacing the entire house with impatient restlessness. However, the true horror of the situation only dawned on her on the third day after the letter was mailed, when Prarthana called from Hyderabad. As soon as she said "Hello," she began to sob so heart-wrenchingly, calling out "Urmi Didi," that she was unable to explain what had happened. Overcome by uncontrollable sobs, she called three times in fifteen minutes and hung up each time. At first, Urmila thought that Debu had caused her some trouble and that she might have to leave for Hyderabad immediately. It was only when Prarthana swallowed her tears and began to speak that Urmila understood what her agony was.
