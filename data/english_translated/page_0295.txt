"Tell me before you leave. We were ready to be together." Urmila was confused; ever since she finished her shift and returned to Shivamogga, from the very moment she arrived, she reminded herself of Himavant's true colors. At no point had he promised her anything, and she had never mistaken him for a ruthless killer. But Himavant no longer carried the same pain as before; there were no agonies or anxieties from the days after Prarthana had left. internal explosions. She realized that she had overlooked this change—the volcanic silence of Himavant had suddenly become calm. She had not guessed the reason. His behavior had become mysterious; even though she was wary, she did not suspect him. Had Himavant changed while being with her? She suddenly turned and looked toward the kitchen. Her intuition was not wrong; a strange fear was dancing in the eyes of Jabeen, who stood at the kitchen door. She felt as though Jabeen could hear and had overheard every word she had spoken with Prarthana. The very next minute, as if deciding that the panic on Jabeen's face should show her the way and that she must find the answer to this problem in those trembling eyes, Urmila stood up. The first question that stirred in her mind was—Where is he? Rasool Jamadar? She did not delay further. At this hour, Himavant would be in Shivamogga, or if not, he would be immersed in the work at the 'Amma Layout' that had started on the Ayanur road. He was completely occupied with work during the day. The work was reaching its final stage. There was another project—the Prarthana Layout work in the Gadikoppa fields.
