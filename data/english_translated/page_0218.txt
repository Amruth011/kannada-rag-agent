The pleasant weather of Rona brought a new sense of liveliness. After finishing her bath, she kissed Debu’s forehead with her petals-of-parijata lips. They shared a cup of steaming coffee. For some reason, all the weariness and bitterness of the night had vanished, and a new longing had sprouted in both of them. After many days, Debu was dressed like a pure Bengali in a white jubba and tight pyjamas. Prarthana had emerged immersed in a pure silk saree, smelling of the earth. He kissed her so deeply and for so long that he couldn't pull away. Just as their bodies were about to tune into each other, forgetting all that was old, the first flight to Hyderabad had arrived. Leaving the Mercedes car they had brought from Davanagere at the hotel, they had rushed to the airport in a taxi. There was a sense of wonder in Prarthana’s eyes. "Truly, am I boarding a plane?" The only memory she had was of holding her brother’s hand in the courtyard of their house with the white stone pillars beyond Channarayapatna, looking up at the sky. A small, bright light, like a tiny moving star amidst the clusters of the night, was a plane—had she ever imagined this? She had only felt that perhaps some prince would seat her on a horse, ride off to an unknown direction, reach a palace chamber, and say, "Prarthana, you are mine now." Beyond that, where were the dreams? Back then, when Himavant, who had walked with her all the way from Channarayapatna to Shivamogga, was washing the wound on her broken big toe and bandaging it, she had felt she had found her black-horse prince.

Now, Debu was fastening the airplane seatbelt around her waist. In the eyes of the air hostess, there was a faint flicker—from where to where had Prarthana arrived? As the plane kicked off the ground and stood in the sky, rising higher and higher, a sunbeam piercing through the window began to play golden games on Debu’s cheeks. A man so handsome, so wealthy, so intelligent—could he not be even better? "Debu?" she said. "What?" "We will always hold each other like this, and love each other so intensely that we cannot bear to be apart even for a moment, right?" she asked. "I swear. Above all, you are my wife. love," he exclaimed, leaning toward her. The air hostess, standing at the tip of the curved airplane aisle, turned her face away with a slight impatience. Hyderabad was within sight.
