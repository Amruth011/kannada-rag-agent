When I, who had left, returned before midnight, why should anyone else not have returned from here? - God forbid that doesn't happen, she thought, knocking on the door even more firmly. "Deb," Prarthana said as the latch inside the door clicked. "Oh... Come in, Prarthana," Deb opened the door. The dim blue light that was burning in the house was enough to determine that no one else was inside. Without even bothering to reply to his exclamation, Prarthana walked in. Deb, closing the door behind her, followed. "What is this... like this so late at night?" Deb kept asking in surprise. She didn't answer anything. She knew Deb took a bath in the evenings. At least lukewarm water would be left in the geyser. Thinking that the water would heat up in ten minutes if she switched it on again, Since it was clear she wouldn't speak for a while, Deb also gave up those questions and sat quietly on a chair in the hall. By then, the initial surprise of why she had come so suddenly had passed, and he was sure that Urmile would not return. But his hope that Prarthana would not be able to leave him came true, and Deb's mind, like Chandranath Bandyopadhyay's lustful eyes, began to see a new desire. But what explanation could be given for the incident that happened in the evening? Deb began to think. "Deb Babu, I'll take a bath and come. Fix a drink for me, I have a lot to talk about," Prarthana said, pulling the bathroom door shut from the inside. She began to wonder about him. He is a man. He must be doing a thousand things. A pure adulterer. Besides, has Deb ever hidden anything from her regarding the affairs at Kaveramma's house? Where did he claim to be a person of immense restraint when it came to women?
