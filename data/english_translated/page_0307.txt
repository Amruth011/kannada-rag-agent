Tell me the reason / 302 There is a diagram in his hand, drawn unclearly. Himavant is digging them out one by one. He is now facing his own words and his own sorcery. In black magic, this is called 'stambhana' (immobilization). A destructive ritual is easy, but stambhana is very dangerous. Ill-omened times wait specifically for such opportunities. What if something happens to Himavant? Rasool Jamadar started to get restless. Urmila did not understand anything. She was slightly uneasy that Himavant had left for Hyderabad directly after talking to Prarthana without telling her a word or giving any indication, but his subsequent behavior only caused her anxiety. What is Himavant doing on the mound? Even Rasool is not speaking. Did Prarthana promise to return to him? Is he canceling this destructive ritual to save Debu’s life because of that? If it hadn't been canceled, would Debu have died? Was there a need for someone like Himavant to learn lowly occult practices? Waves of questions rose and stood still in her mind. A small cry of "Hm" was heard. The process of digging on the mound also stopped. If she had gone and looked at Himavant’s condition, his expression, the tragic posture in which he lay flat on the ground, the thin trickle of blood coming out of his mouth, the wounded tongue—what would Urmila’s state have been if she had seen all that? Having retrieved a panchaloha (five-metal) plate from under the final layer, Himavant lay there for a while as if he had lost consciousness. He slowly recovered and gathered all the items he had buried under the wooden planks, tying them into a bundle with a cloth soaked in turmeric water. He felt like he was losing his strength, yet without losing his patience, he dug another small pit and carefully retrieved the mother-of-pearl jewelry and wedding saree he had kept reserved for Prarthana. He tied the ritual materials he had procured from Rasool Jamadar into another cloth. As final knot, a snake-shaped hairpin belonging to his mother flashed in the afternoon sun. The memory of Prarthana pinched him. Letting out a small cry, he called Rasool and ordered him to take the three bundles and place them in the backseat of the car. His face was now as calm as the sky after the rain. Back to the well.
