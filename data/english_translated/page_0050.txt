Himavant had become a man of sorrow, a being devoid of hope, since he had left the college in Davanagere. He had become as brittle as a piece of graphite, and his eyes had lost their sparkle, just like Prarthana, who had not been able to visit him even once since he had moved to the farm. The middle-class people of Shivamogga would gather around him, mesmerized by his calm demeanor and the sincerity in his eyes, and their faith in him would increase. As he sat there, watching the trucks carrying cement, sand, and stones, he would mutter to himself, "What's the use of building?" His hand would tremble with excitement, but he knew that he couldn't bring himself to take in the homeless man who had been left behind, like a discarded piece of cloth.

He had built a small room on top of the godown he had constructed, and it was his only refuge.
