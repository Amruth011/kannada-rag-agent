Not at all, look at his eyes. They were not just the eyes of someone sleep-deprived; what kind of eyes were those, Himu? But what is visible today is not what I saw then. For some reason, Himavant looks agitated and sorrowful. She had come with the intention of staying with him for a few days, comforting him, and returning to her studies once his mind was under control. But now, she is feeling afraid. He does not look like a sorrowful man; those are not the eyes of a person who has lost someone. He is behaving as if he is someone who cannot stand the presence of human beings, as if he cannot bear the shadow of a woman. Himu was never like this before. There is nothing more terrifying than a person like Himavant becoming a misanthrope. It is as if his words have dried up and his mind has died. Is this happening because of the shock Prarthana left him with? Does Himavant need treatment? This time, when returning from Davangere, she thought she should speak with the psychiatry professor, Rudrappa.

"When are you leaving?" Himu asked again.

"Look, Himavant, my exams start in ten days. I have already finished reading quite a lot, and there is nothing much left to attend to in terms of classes. All that is left is to sit and study. Even if you say no, I will not listen. I will go to Davangere today, get my clothes and books, and come back. I will go and return on the days of the exams. Not for your sake, Himavant; for my own sake, I need to stay here for a month. Don't say that I shouldn't be here since I am the one who has gotten us involved," she said.

Himavant did not speak. Only when she stood ready to leave for town did he come to the edge of the porch, and said, "Don't feel bad. I have no objection if you want to come and stay."

Urmila suddenly turned back, held his hand tightly, squeezed it, leaned forward, and kissed his forearm. In the backyard of the stone house, a calf mooed loudly. *Amba!* Had an unspoken love sprouted?

After that, some events took place in Davangere. By the time Urmila returned from Shivamogga, Prarthana had already moved all of her things from the hostel room to somewhere else, and Urmila was preparing to permanently move to Debu’s house in Channarayapatna. She did not feel like talking to her about anything, even though they had been friends for two years and she was younger than her. Right or wrong?
