Himavant and Prarthana were lying down, but neither of them had the chance to sleep. One of them turned back and used Prarthana as a pillow; tonight, for this letter, you must answer me in these sleepless minutes, young one. Lying in the same bed, Himavant was thinking about how to say it to Prarthana, and just then, he whispered to her, "Himoo, do you love me so much that you would tell me if I am in trouble?" Turning towards him, Prarthana listened to him for a moment in that corner. Time stood still. Himavant, instead of answering her, slowly wrapped his arms around her and pulled her towards him. His warm breath, the rise and fall of his strong chest, and the scent of the earth that clung to his body, completely enveloped Prarthana. "Himoo, Himoo," he whispered, his voice trembling. Prarthana, with a tender touch, buried her face in the folds of his shirt, her body swaying with the rhythm of their hearts beating together. For the rest of the night, they did not sleep. But when Prarthana woke up to the morning light, there was a small note on the trunk of the tree, tied with a mallige flower. Himavant got up quickly. "Oh, Prarthana, the devil has turned into a rakshasa. What is this sleepless night? But we have enough devotion and faith to ward off the rakshasa from both of us. The touch of each other is now the only thing that can save us. Prarthana, you are not just a body; touch. You are the sum of all these things. Doctor, Prarthana is needed. I have to live until our curse is broken, until the name of the sin is erased from my letter. Oh, bear the weight of this answer. Prarthana wrapped herself in a warm blanket.
