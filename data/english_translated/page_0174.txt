Urmila, standing at the threshold, gasped in shock and leaned against the wall. The woman at the doorway, Urmila, stood there, her eyes intense, a line of sweat trickling down her forehead. No, the woman from Mangalore didn't dare to look at Urmila’s face properly. Debu, slumped in his chair, had turned into a statue. What a tragic silence had descended upon the small house’s hall. It felt as if if anyone of the three spoke, it wouldn't be a mere word, but would explode like a bomb. Leaning against the wall, after looking at the tall figure, she looked at Debu once. "A man? You're the one she loves? This disgust wells up from the depths of my being. Urmi, I want you. My dealings are always straightforward. I don't have the patience to build dreams of love and wait. You are my love-time friend. A small encounter is enough for such dealings to be over. If I had asked directly, whether I would have agreed to be with you or not, that's a different matter. But listen, I'll tell you the truth: the kind of disgust I feel for you now, it definitely wouldn't have surfaced then. I told you that at the Forest Lodge gate. If you still want me, then let it all happen. But what did you say that day? What did you say until yesterday afternoon? Is it your deceit or self-deceit, you tell me, Debu. You call it love, you call it affection. You say you’ve stayed with me, dying and living. You say your sincere love for me hasn't died even after ten years. Now tell me, Prarthana is dying. You say it's just to hurt me. Even after looking at you with such intense eyes, you expect me to believe you and your words?" Her angry words flowed from one sentence to another. Urmila felt something was burning inside her, she didn't know what. If she had been any more patient, perhaps she would have managed to bear everything. But the doorbell rang. A man. Urmila couldn't bear to look at the girl’s helpless face. Debu couldn't muster the courage to look at the girl’s face, let alone the Mangalore woman he had called over. She must have had a drink. It’s been at most two hours since she arrived.
