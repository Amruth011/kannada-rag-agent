Prarthana, who had been pushed by an unconscious urge to stand up beside a mound in the middle of the barren, desolate field, was shivering in the darkness. Debu’s arrival was unexpected. There was no doubt or hesitation in her mind; she had decided that after staying with Himavant for this one night, she would explain her decision to him, seek his blessing, and leave for Davanagere as soon as dawn broke to meet Debu. Was that all? Had she not, even once, longed to experience that indescribable sense of security—lying on the same mat as Himavant, just as they had for so many years, with no line drawn between them, resting her head on his arm—once more? Her inner mind questioned her; had Debu’s car not arrived, scattering the thick darkness with its headlights, perhaps she would have answered 'yes'. From the moment they met until this very minute, she had been receiving everything she needed from Himavant. He had given everything without ever asking for anything in return, like a god. He gave her courage, gave her the opportunity to walk towards her goals, gave her sleep in his eyes even when he lay right beside her, gave her the canopy of moonlight, gave morsels to her hungry mind, and gave peace to her heart when it felt like she was being torn apart. In these two and a half years, he had poured everything he had into her, yet Himavant's vessel never ran dry. If she had asked him to hold her close again like he used to, to spend one more night in that same silent, loving way... Himavant would not have said no to that either. Because a god’s palms never empty. She was the truly unfortunate one, standing there having taken everything! Was it the moisture in Prarthana’s eyes, a change in her decision, or the pressure of circumstances? Life had brought her and left her standing in this desolate field. Two years ago, didn't she dream of catching a glimpse of this same Himavant? Wasn't she in such a state that she couldn't bear to be away from him for even a second? Hadn't she wept a thousand times, asking if this life was destined for her? When Himavant would gently pat her body, which was like a tuned veena, and lay her down with cool, soothing words, hadn't she burned with desire and impatience? Her body had not known what lust was. The only one who knew was Himavant.
