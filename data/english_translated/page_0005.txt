I have been wandering around the country, and my weight, which was once around one hundred and six kilograms, has now reduced to sixty-nine kilograms. It all started with the interview of Kola Fayaz, the murderer from Shivajinagar, and my journey in journalism, which has taken me to the depths of Italy, has been a long one. I have come to realize that writing without investigation, research, and emotion is not worth it, and it has taken me years to understand this. I have traveled to more than thirty countries and have written about various topics such as love, history, lust, war, terrorism, cinema, and more. I have been fortunate enough to have been given the Aktha award, and I am content.

I have also ventured into television, and I am the executive officer of Janashree TV. It took me twenty-four years to receive my first Sahitya Akademi award, and I have also received the Shivaram Karanth Award, the Masti Award, and the Rajyotsava Award. I have translated the autobiography of a Telugu writer, and I have received the Kuvempu Bhasha Bharati Award for it. The biggest award I have received is from the auto drivers of Bangalore, who have given me the "Sakkathaga Bariythaane" award.

I have also acted in a few Kannada films, and I have written several books, including "Haay Bangalore" and "Omanase". I have a book store in Gandhi Bazaar, Bangalore, called "BBC" (Belagere Book Center and Coffee). I have a mix of fans and critics, and I have two daughters and two sons, as well as two wives and four grandchildren. My weaknesses include smoking, traveling, reading, writing, music, and history. The world is my home, and I am always wondering what to do next.
