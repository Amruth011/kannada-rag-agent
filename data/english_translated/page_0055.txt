There she was, a girl who had grown up in the lap of her parents, Himavant and Prarthana, in Shivamogga; Channarayapatna's daughter, a girl who had entered the hostel for the first time and was crying; there was nothing special about her. In Prarthana's room, the senior girl, who had not yet spoken a word, was sitting on her bed, looking like a Khaisoori from a village. The house was full of doctors. Ah, mother; my brother and sister (she had been brought up believing that she too would become a doctor like them); she had come to Davaangere and joined the hostel, and for the first time, she was feeling a bit excited; she was studying medicine, and it was not like studying Michaelangelo's art; she was reading a review of it, sitting in her room, but she couldn't afford to take her eyes off her books. As the days passed, she would get tired and sleep, but she had made up her mind to study from the very first day; she would get up early in the morning and get ready to go to college, and when she came out, she would wear a neat and tidy cotton dress, and tie her hair in a ponytail, and go out; she was a different person when she came back in the evening, wearing a pair of trousers and a T-shirt, and carrying a small swimming trunk in her hand; she would tie her hair in a ponytail and go out; she would walk in the corridors of the hostel, wearing slippers, and people would look at her with envy; she didn't know anything about the world. On Friday nights, she would extinguish all the lights in her room, light a small candle, and mix gin and lemon in a card, and drink it; that night, she would smoke two cigarettes, but on other nights, she would only smoke one; she had never talked about boys with Prarthana; why was that? She was like any other girl from Davaangere; no one had ever seen her talking to boys; she was considered a strange girl, and people would say, "But Prarthana, what's wrong with you?" "I just want to live," she would say, "I don't need a husband; I don't need to cook for anyone; I don't need children; I don't have any enemies."
