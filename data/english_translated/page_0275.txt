She wondered if she had settled in. She thought that once a couple of days passed, Jabeen's unfamiliarity and silence might fade away or improve. Himavant finished his dinner quickly and went to bed. Urmila also fell asleep. It must have been one o'clock in the morning when suddenly, someone rattled the outside gate. Then, the sound of a car starting was heard. Peering through her room window, she could only see the two red tail lights of the car as it sped away. "It wasn't Himavant who drove off," Urmila told herself. Because the car did not leave with the speed of a novice driver. Or rather, it had been only five days since Himavant had learned to drive; why would he hide that from her? She had been lying there thinking about just that. Wasn't it in the room opposite that Himavant was sleeping? Remembering this, she opened her room door without making a sound and stepped out. She just touched the door of his room with her fingertips; it opened. As she had guessed, Himavant’s bed was empty. As she turned back towards her room, her eyes suddenly froze. Jabeen was standing in the corner of the hall. Her eyes did not look like those of someone who had just woken up from sleep. She was staring fixedly at Urmila. There were no questions there. No surprise. It was just an observation. "It seems Himavant went somewhere, right?" Urmila asked. Jabeen remained in stony silence. "Didn't you get any sleep?" she asked again. Jabeen did not even blink. For some reason, Urmila suddenly started feeling more like she had come and stayed at a stranger's house rather than Himavant's. Himavant, whom she had seen a few days ago, the Himavant who had collapsed, shattered by pain—he felt more familiar. This did not even seem like recovery to her. She thought, a hurt soul becomes more guarded. That is natural; especially for women—she consoled herself and tried to sleep. When Himavant returned home, he said, "What to do, tell me? A cement load arrived suddenly in the night. I thought of going to pick you up, but you must have been asleep, so I thought, why wake you? I went alone."
