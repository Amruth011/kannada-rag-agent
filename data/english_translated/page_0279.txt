The reason | 274 If one does not go away—first the person experiencing separation feels a surge of grief. Gradually, for some support, loneliness seeks a foothold. A deep, unbearable sorrow, where is the woman who feels it? Someone says; then the man in the world swears lifelong vows; promises; love, companionship, he calls it love. The feeling that one is making a mistake intensifies. The girl, while melting away, cannot return. When the first love haunts, even if one returns to that place, it is already too late. In a world of billions... is Himavant’s love dying like this? He took his hand off hers. Love is not something that dies in this lifetime; it might be the sweat on Urmila’s forehead. But the life-veena (the lute of life) for Prarthana does not exist even for a moment. Because, today hatred rules; is it possible for love to die? Love will not lean towards a hollow life. That broken sacrifice has already begun in this lifetime. She gathered herself and sat up. "Sorry, I have lost," she thought, as if it remained in her lap. The scent of Urmila’s body still lingered in the car. Neither of them spoke until they reached Jog. They drove the car into the shade of a tree and went inside the traveler’s bungalow. By the time they finished washing, Himavant remembered Prarthana, who had not eaten anything since morning. Urmila said, "I will cook and serve you myself." It was clear that she was a guest in the bungalow. Himavant did not react. The meaning in her words was... They walked out of the room quickly; the roar of the waterfall was audible. The water seemed to leap from a height and fall to the ground, as if jumping—slowly and naturally. Urmila held Himavant’s hand. They began to descend into the valley amidst the mist. They walked in absolute silence, occasionally speaking, occasionally making space for each other, and laughing heartily at small talk. Suddenly, for some reason, Urmila’s fingers gripped Himavant’s shoulder. In the excitement of descending into the valley, she once raised her arm to hug his shoulder, another time she was stroking his back, sometimes she leaned against him, or wrapped her arms around him. Himavant, looking tired, leaned against a large boulder. Urmila, standing beside him, thought, 'I am a creeper.' By the time they climbed back up from the depths of the valley, the sun had begun to fade.
