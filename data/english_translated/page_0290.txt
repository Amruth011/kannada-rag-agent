"Tell me the reason, or just leave. Don't marry Debu and share his curse. We are his parents... whatever happens, whatever we have to endure, we must bear it. If we can't do anything, he is in Hyderabad, at the Golconda Hotel, with a man called Nishani Fakeer. We will come, you just leave, daughter." Chandranath Bandopadhyay had spoken in a choked voice, having made Prarthana lie down in the inner room. Prarthana sat silently for a long time, then got up and went to Debu’s side. He was already burning with fever. She wiped the sore near his lip with a wet cloth, then placed her hand on his forehead. "Prarthana..." he groaned softly without pain. She sat on the bed, lifted his head, and placed it on her lap. It felt like holding a burning ember. She wet another piece of cloth, wrung it out, and spread it soothingly over his forehead. It was getting dark outside. She switched on the light. Still holding Debu on her lap, she closed her eyes and began reciting her evening shlokas after so many months: "Ya Kundendu Tushara Hara Dhavala..." Debu's father, standing at the door, began to sob silently. The night had begun like that.

"Why do you doubt? I have seen it with my own eyes. They call it Lilavati Hospital. They brought him to ward number 358. They said they would provide different treatment. His mother put a drop of water in his mouth—look—and just like that, he breathed his last!" Rasool Jamadar was describing every detail of Sujay Malhotra's final moments without missing anything. Himavant sat on the stone floor of the house in the Gadikoppa fields, listening to it all without showing a single reaction. The night was very dense. Rasool Jamadar also looked exhausted. There was not a trace of sadness or remorse in Rasool Saab for Sujay Malhotra's death, or for the fact that he himself was directly responsible for it. However, on the day he had drawn a blood-red rangoli in front of Prarthana's house in Davanagere, he did not seem to be his usual self. He had hesitantly muttered, "This shouldn't have happened..."
