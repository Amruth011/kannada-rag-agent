Prarthana does not yet understand the extent of his sacrifice, nor does she realize how his life has blossomed like a flower overnight. She is lost in her own excitement. Thinking this, Urmila caressed Prarthana’s cheek. For some reason, Urmila felt that way; perhaps because of the situation. "There is nothing I can do about this. Everything must be left to the decision of time. How time changes our lives, isn't it, Didi? Previously, when you both were talking in the car, I would sit quietly in the back seat. Now, Debu is sitting in the front, and the steering wheel is in his hands. You are standing behind me, blessing me. Aren't you, Didi? By this time tomorrow, the engagement will be over. If Debu agrees, I will vacate the hostel and move into his house. The date we went to his parents' place is drawing near. If I am with Debu, he will guide me very well. He is a brilliant fellow, of course. After shifting to that house, I will miss you a lot, Didi. I don't know if I am a good person or not, but I am certainly not a bad one. I might have made mistakes without knowing. You will forgive me, won't you, Didi? You must be with me tomorrow. Don't get angry with Debu and push me away." As she spoke in an excited tone while lying on Prarthana's lap, Urmila felt like asking her, "Don't you remember Himavant even once?" But she didn't ask, because she realized she was remembering Himavant more often than Prarthana was. He probably didn't even know about this matter. In the house behind the Gundi Chowltry in Davanagere, with Prarthana, Debu, and both their parents all gathered together, they were writing and signing a tragic fate for Himavant. Unbeknownst to him, she was the only one left here to shed tears on his behalf! Swallowing the exclamation, "So sorry, Himu," Urmila fell silent. Urmila stood in amazement, watching the way Prarthana behaved the next day, the changes in her demeanor, and their engagement, which proceeded as if nothing could stop it. By the time the day dawned, Prarthana was already dressed in a brand-new peacock-colored silk saree, with her hair braided loosely, and on her forehead...
