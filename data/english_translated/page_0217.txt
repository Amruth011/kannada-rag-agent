As the car continued to cross the stretch, Prarthana asked in a voice that sounded quite natural, "Does Shivarajayya uncle have a daughter?" Debu was startled, but he didn't show it. "He had one," he replied briefly. "What do you mean 'had'? Doesn't she exist now?" Prarthana asked. "No; she died. She killed herself." "Committed suicide?" she asked, her voice rising and falling. "Why?" "God knows. Why does everyone commit suicide? They do it when they are bored with life; she must have done the same." Saying this, he stopped. "What was her name?" Prarthana asked persistently. "Some stupid name. Sarvamangala, I think. Let it go. It's a good thing she died. So ugly. Forget it, why bring that up now? Change the cassette, Mukesh is becoming boring. For a journey, only Kishore is right. By the way, had you gone to see Shivarajayya?" he asked abruptly. "Just like that... to thank him," Prarthana said. "Don't repeat this. Don't go anywhere without my knowledge; I don't like it," he said, and after that, he didn't speak a single word until they reached Bengaluru. For the first time, Debu felt that she was beginning her life with a murderer. As she stepped out of the car in front of the Windsor Manor hotel in Bengaluru, she felt like taking a bus back to Davanagere—no, to Shivamogga—and just leaving. She couldn't go. It rained very heavily in Bengaluru that night. Wearing a nightie, Prarthana stood by the wide-open window, watching the rain. That was when Debu felt she was like a sitar, and the question arose whether he should play it. But he felt as though he had become numb, unable to even rise from the bed he was lying on. This was because two names kept haunting him repeatedly: Sarvamangala and Sujay Kulhotra.

By the next morning, Prarthana had woken up with a completely different mindset at Windsor Manor.
