Just as he had looked at Appa and the sister-in-law, Debu and me today—that was the feeling. The question of whether he was any different from Appa did not even cross his mind. Himavant felt it would have been better if he had tied me down himself. Before he could even think that, Debu came to mind. The love for him was pure. He was mine, only mine. If there were no property, no status, no beauty; would I have loved Debu even without those? Wealth, status—none of these exist in Himavant, and perhaps that is the very reason I turned towards Debu. If a situation were to arise where Debu suddenly had none of these, could I change my mind? Would I still love him then? Yes... I would love him. Now, life, wealth, status, and respect—all of these belong to Himavant and are dedicated to him. If he were to lose them suddenly, would my love turn towards him again? No... he is a god; I worship him even today. With that, Prarthana reached a decision; her love for Debu became even more intense. After visiting Himavant—especially after seeing him in that state—there had been a small anxiety that a fondness for him might arise in her again and that she might change. That fear subsided. He would always be a god, and I will worship the god, she thought and closed her eyes.

Urmila, who had woken up in the morning and sat chanting slokas, came to the backyard and saw Himavant sitting there, hugging a calf, stroking its body, and feeding it tender, light green grass. Thinking, "Isn't this calf's name Prarthana?", she cautioned herself not to accidentally say that name out loud. However, unaware that Urmila was standing behind him, Himavant, in an extremely gentle and affectionate voice, said, "Prarthana..." As he said it, he leaned forward and kissed the calf's cheek. The bell around its neck tinkled softly. The mother cow suddenly turned and looked. "How innocently a man who loves can love, Himavant! At most, one becomes a husband. But your personality is different: you become a friend, a mother, a relative, a god, a guru, a guide... you become everything," Urmila said.

Himavant turned around suddenly and asked, "When are you going to your hometown?" Urmila was startled.
