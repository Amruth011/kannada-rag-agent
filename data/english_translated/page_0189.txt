But Sujay Malboh's memory killed his desires. Forget about getting married; his mind didn't even dare to touch her. The rest of the night was completely empty. Somewhere outside the house, the third watch of the night was turning. He covered Prarthana for the night with a blanket and lay down on a mat on the floor. Just as sleep was about to take hold, he suddenly sat up. "What exactly did Sujay Malboh do to you?" he asked in a clear voice, but it felt more like he was asking himself than Prarthana. Without the slightest inkling that in his future life, he would have to wake up like this in the third watch of many nights and wear himself out by repeatedly asking this same question, Debashish Chandrakant Bandopadhyay had fallen asleep that day. A different answer, completely unrelated to his question, had enveloped his inner being by morning. "No, this is not adultery; it's not flirting. If I hadn't loved Prarthana with all my heart, this fact that she rode on a bike embracing him would not have bothered me so much; I would not have been so wounded. Why any doubts about this?" I love Prarthana. And love Prarthana. Debu's lust, Debu the flirt, from last night... died! Just as he decided this, Prarthana came out from the inner room. The day was shining.

Prarthana's eyes were serene. The anxieties of the night had vanished. After her bath, wearing Debu's lungi and t-shirt again, she came out of the bathroom with her wet hair falling down her back. She was an absolute nymph. Debu was staring at her. She wasn't exceptionally clever. She had just enough intelligence to get into a medical course.
