Although there was no sign of him returning to consciousness, the strange symptoms of his illness began to disappear one by one inside the bungalow. Himavant had forbidden the doctors from visiting, stating that there was a rule that no one should remain inside the bungalow. The compound watchman did not enter the bungalow, and likewise, no one was allowed to enter the room where Himavant remained, except for Prarthana. Himavant spent most of his time performing rituals sitting in front of Debu. Whenever he got a break, he would go and sit in the backyard garden. Now, Prarthana kept watch over his every move. Since the day he arrived at the bungalow, his fever had not decreased even slightly. It felt as if his physical strength was declining day by day. However, he spoke a lot with Prarthana. Between the moments of big laughter and eyes welling up with tears that would vanish within the lashes, they talked about when they met two and a half years ago, their falling in love, and her leaving from Shivamogga to Davanagere—as if nothing else related to Debu had happened since then. Sometimes, even Debu would come to their memory. For the first two days, Prarthana felt uneasy. Then, gradually, she started acting as if the talk regarding Debu had nothing to do with her. She did every task for Himavant herself. As long as he was inside Debu's room, Prarthana would also remain in front of Debu. Everyone in the bungalow, except for Debu, knew that she was sleeping in Himavant's room at night, but she hadn't even told Urmila about the fact that they were sleeping on the same mat. Since everyone else was anxious only for Debu's life to be saved and for him to return to consciousness, none of them bothered about Prarthana’s behavior. But only one figure in that bungalow was agitated: full of anxiety. Seeing Prarthana and Himavant sitting leaning against each other on a stone bench in the backyard of the bungalow, peering from a distant window, the figure said to itself: "There are no signs of Debu regaining consciousness. Something else is going on." The name of the figure who said that was Urmila.
