Urmila stood at the door of the stone house, watching him. She felt that the surprise was not on her face. When Prarthana came to Davangere, she had come with the intention of telling Debu something about Himavant. The moment she got out of the car and ran into the hostel zone, crying out, "Urmila Didi," and sobbing into her arms, Urmila had felt that something terrible had happened. Debu had sent word from the visitors' room; she had no enthusiasm to get up and go see him, yet she had laid the sobbing Prarthana on the bed and gone. Standing at the door of the visitors' room with guilty eyes, Debu Babu did not say much. "Urmila, every time I come to stand before you like this, I am a culprit. This time too, father. Prarthana will give you the details; please console her. She is badly hurt. I knew this would happen. But Prarthana did not listen to me. She went alone. I just followed her."

"Did you go to that Shivamogga that you stumbled upon that day?" Urmila had asked abruptly. He had nodded as if to say yes. Urmila had returned to her room as if she could not stand there for even one more moment. She did not need any explanation from Prarthana. Because, once she knew that the two of them had been to the fields of Gaadikoppa, Urmila could imagine the kind of devastation that might have occurred there. She tried to console Prarthana, who was crying as if her heart would break, but suddenly she fell silent, thinking that one shouldn't console those who have been destroyed.

In fact, it seemed Himavant did not react at all. At first, he acted as if the things Prarthana told him were not even registered in his mind or thoughts. He spoke like someone who had lost all sense of rhythm. Later, he stayed silent as if nothing had happened. He had collapsed on the wounds and mounds of Gaadikoppa, crying, but then recovered immediately and walked away. He laid out the bedding for these two, put away the vessels, and washed Debu’s feet. "It is not all that, Urmila Didi. Why are you doing this? Why are you leaving me? Why are you marrying him?"—the words that even Prarthana could not ask, Himavant asked as if someone else was speaking through his throat.
