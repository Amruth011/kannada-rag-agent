Doctor, that chapter is finished, that night has passed. If not into your hands, into whose hands should I leave my half-fulfilled dream? I am still here; my candy shop is still here. If I fall while being stubborn, the dream will not continue; for today, I am free. Give me permission to leave. These are the documents related to the nursing home. The car parked outside is in the Doctor’s name. Even the calf in the backyard is named Prarthana; only my half-fulfilled dream didn't get a name. I am leaving. Saying this, he looked at Prarthana and Debu to his heart's content one last time. In the movement of his tear-filled eyes, both images became blurred. Unable to stand, Debu felt dizzy, and Prarthana stood by to support him. After a while, leaving him under the care of Rasool Jamadar, she came and stood before Himavant. For a moment, their eyes met. Then, seeing her bow down and touch her forehead to Himavant’s feet, tears welled up in Debu’s eyes. After Himavant left that place, to everyone there, the entire expanse of Gadikoppa began to feel like a deserted night, like a half-fulfilled dream. They stood still like statues until the calf named Prarthana, tied in the backyard, let out a loud "Amba."

It sounded as if someone knocked on the door. When Himavant, who was in the darkness, came and opened the room door, a ray of light from the large lamps of the Shivamogga circle fell upon the face of the woman standing in the courtyard, dancing. He did not speak a single word; he brought her into the room and closed the door. In the darkness under the Sampige tree outside the compound, Rasool Jamadar’s figure let out a sigh of relief. The arrival of another dream had begun.
