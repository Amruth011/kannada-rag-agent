It was not possible in the darkness. Likewise, the conversation ended there. As Himavant began to walk toward Urmila, Prarthana could not think of any other questions to ask him. She felt it would be impossible to discuss that subject with Himavant again. A proud man who had closed the doors of his heart! "Himavant, if you don't mind, I would like to stay with you for a few days. I have exams; I will go to Davangere tomorrow to bring my clothes and books. on the days of my exams, write them, and come back. I will just study quietly and stay with you, please." Urmila spoke, surprised herself at how those words had come to her so suddenly. Himavant did not answer. She had exams that day as well; she hadn't even been in a state to bring her own books and clothes. He had brought them himself. To ensure she wouldn't stay awake, he would stay awake with her, reading something. She would go and write her exams, and he would drop her off. Sometimes she would study on her own. He had stirred up the dreams himself. Urmila was now saying she would come and stay with him for a few days. He understood her love and her deep concern, but the one thing Urmila herself did not understand was that every attempt she made to help him forget Prarthana only reminded him of Prarthana. Not just her; no matter which girl in the world came and told him she would stay with him, he would only remember Prarthana, and it would only increase his hatred toward her. As far as this life was concerned, that was the ultimate truth. As the darkness deepened, Urmila held Himavant’s hand and walked on. Even after reaching the outskirts of Gadi Koppa, he did not answer the question Urmila had asked. Therefore, Urmila had decided that even if he said no, it was best for her to come and stay with Himavant for a few days—in this open field, in the forest, or wherever he was. However, a few hours after eating and falling asleep, Himavant woke up without waking Urmila. Just like the first day, he crawled toward the iron trunk, using both his hands and legs like a clean-limbed person. If Urmila had seen the expression on his face at that moment, her decisions would have changed that very day!
