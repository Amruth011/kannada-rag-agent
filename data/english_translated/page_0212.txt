The large *kumkum* on her forehead seemed to be competing with her smile. As soon as the hostel warden told her that someone had come to meet her, she said to herself, "It must be Himavant," and rushed out of the room. It was him, indeed. She stood under the neem tree in the premises and spoke briefly with her father. The excitement of seeing her father after such a long time, the restlessness of having taken so many decisions without his knowledge, the eagerness to inquire about her mother, her elder brother, and especially her sister-in-law—none of that surfaced. Remaining completely detached, she spoke only four words and returned to her room. She had given him some money and instructed him to buy a new shirt and come to the Gundi Chatra by ten o'clock. She had already purchased a light yellow shirt and a dark blue pant for Debu from Shivamogga, even though he wasn't there. Was her behavior throughout the day the practicality of a very clever woman, or the last-minute cruelty of a heartless girl? Urmila could not decide.

After the engagement ceremony, as she was returning to the hostel after bidding farewell to everyone, the watchman at the reception handed Prarthana a packet. Inside it was the coconut candy that Prarthana loved the most. Written in bold letters on the packet was: "I had come to Davanagere; I am returning just like that. I will not disturb you. Exams are near; study well. Your Himavant." As soon as she went to her room, Prarthana collapsed onto her bed and began to wail.
