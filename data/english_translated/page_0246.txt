Are they two sides of the same coin? Or a deception? My life for these past two and a half years—for her, solely for her. If so, was all of that selfishness? I had set everything aside—a house for her; a hospital swing in the cow within a shelter calf for her... Was doing all that selfish? No, I did all that for her with the same devotion a human has for their God. God is a hypocrite. God is selfish; he accepts everything, yet bestows nothing. miser. God is merciless. bastard! No, Prarthana is a bitch! From today, from this very moment, I must hate her! As soon as he decided that, Himavant felt disappointed for some reason. It felt as if a small spark of energy surged through his body. The anger that had started small spread throughout his body in no time. He stood up abruptly; there was no trace of the early morning weakness in his legs. It felt like everything was under his control. He walked to the backyard, untied the calf, and milked the cow. For some reason, he made coffee with great care and drank it. The sun seemed to be blazing. Instead of switching on the electric water heater, he felt like lighting a traditional stove and taking an oil bath. As he sat down to light the stove, he suddenly remembered something; he ran quickly, opened a cupboard door, and brought out a bundle of Prarthana’s letters that he had kept with the utmost care for two and a half years. He didn't even feel like reading them one more time. He began tossing them into the stove’s fire with great devotion. Only when he was about to push the very last letter into the fire, his hand trembled. Prarthana had written the single line, "Can anyone ever deceive God?" all across the letter. "I don't know. God becomes a demon, Prarthana!" Himavant said to himself in a loud voice and threw that last letter into the water stove too. By the time it was completely burnt to ash and every letter of Prarthana’s had melted and evaporated, a strange, unprecedented energy surged through Himavant's body. If love, the kind of love that exists, takes an entire night to reduce a grown man to a mere worm, then doesn't it take only a few minutes for the petty feeling of hatred—consisting of only two and a half letters—to fill that same man, who was in a helpless, exhausted state, with titanic strength? Truly, I... so wholeheartedly Prarthana...
