The joy on the faces of Debu’s parents was unmistakable. They were immersed in praising her; the taste of the meal Prarthana had prepared was still lingering on their tongues. The cleanliness of the house, her soft-spoken words, the reflection of her upbringing in every gesture—the couple had clearly reached the decision that Debu had not made a mistake in choosing this girl. He was like a child playing in the celestial path. After the meal, Prarthana came down and went into the inner room. After laying out the beds for Debu’s parents in the hall and for Debu outside, she sprinkled a little water to settle the dust, served them water to drink, and said, "I will head to the hostel now." No matter how much they insisted, she refused to stay the night. Even when Debu offered to drop her off at the hostel, she stopped him. "It has been months since the three of you met, sit and talk comfortably. I have some studying to do tonight. Dad is coming in the morning, and I will be here by breakfast time. How far is the hostel from here? There are streetlights all along the way; in front of the iron gate in five minutes. Since I already told them I would be late, the watchman will be waiting at the door." Saying this, she smiled at the elderly couple once more and left.

"How did you manage to cook all that? There wasn't even a proper vessel in the house!" Debu asked in a whisper as he walked her to the house gate.

"Debu Babu, poor girls know the art of creating a meal out of nothing. More than that, love makes everything possible. By the way, I had to tell you one thing: your mom and dad are much nicer than you!" Saying this, Prarthana walked briskly toward the hostel.

By the time Debu recovered from her remark, some time had passed. As Prarthana walked out of Debu’s house, two sharp eyes began to watch her. Like a python that has tasted blood circling a dove's nest again and again, those eyes followed Prarthana’s trail. Prarthana knocked on the latch of the iron gate and stood waiting for the old hostel watchman to come. The sharp eyes moved closer. "Prarthana, I have been waiting for you since evening. Love you."
