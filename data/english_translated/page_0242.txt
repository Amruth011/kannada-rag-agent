'Tell me the reason,' he stood there like stone. He must have left Davangere that very morning; the dust from the journey seemed to have dulled the color of that expensive car. He suddenly went and brought two large buckets of water, washed the Mercedes until it glistened like a child of the sun, and wiped it clean with his own dhoti. After finishing his bath and eating breakfast, he emerged from the stone house dressed in crisp clothes. Debu was smiling even more than Prarthana. "He is the right one!" Channarayapatna exclaimed to himself. As the two of them walked toward the car, Prarthana exclaimed once more, "You look great!" She bowed and touched his feet in reverence. Himavant stood impassively, like a tree that had endured a lightning strike. When shaking Himavant’s hands, which were as rough as tree bark, Debu’s palms were sweating; they were trembling. Neither of them spoke. Debu started the car, and Prarthana sat in the passenger seat. For some reason, suddenly, the cute white calf named Prarthana in the backyard of the stone house let out a tragic, mournful cry, "Ambaaa!" Prarthana began to cry. As the car pulled out of the open field of Gadikoppa and turned toward Shivamogga, she looked back once. Himavant was still standing there, like a lonely boat. It seemed as if his entire body was trembling as if it were about to shatter. The moment the car was about to disappear from sight, his right hand rose and fluttered feebly in the air. One word, one syllable, one sentence had remained stuck in his throat. It was a question that Himavant, as a man, had chosen to remain silent about, as if he could never ask the girl he loved ever again. "Tell me the reason?" He would never ask. After the car disappeared, for some reason, his body suddenly felt light, blood circulation began again, his fingertips grew warm, and everything seemed to return to a state of normalcy. As if he had decided long ago, Himavant went inside that same stone house. Without succumbing to any emotional outburst, he opened the lock of the iron chest. He didn't have to search for anything before his eyes. For how many years, months, weeks, and days had he been looking at that same iron chest? Every corner of it was familiar; he knew exactly where every object was kept. With both hands, he lifted that small bundle out of the chest. Once he untied it and looked at everything inside to his heart's content, only then did his lips tremble.
