Was it when the bullet fired? Was it when she was brought down from the terrace to the room's door? Was it when, on the Gajanur area, Mother was remembered as if from the depths of her navel? He could not remember exactly. But that day, Prarthana had been walking just like this; embracing herself—with the same affection, the same helplessness, the same slowness! She is the same even today; only the shape of the figure embracing her shoulder has changed. Every human being in the world keeps changing the shapes beside their shoulders like this. When the person who embraces moves away, they stand in the place of the person who observes; that is why it hurts. That is all. That is what love is; what betrayal is; what disappointment is—the sum total of it all is just pain. The pain that Prarthana is moving away from him, the pain that she is becoming Debu’s share—what is the difference between the two? Thinking this, Himavant stood firmly, planting his feet. The two of them were close. Loosening the hand that was embracing Prarthana, Debu reached out towards him, saying, "Hello Himavant." It felt like touching the tip of an iceberg. It felt as if the mighty man named Himavant had suddenly died long ago. Debu immediately pulled his hand back; he felt that what Prarthana said was true—Himavant was in deep shock. Not knowing what to talk to him about, he stood there. He began to feel that he, even more than Prarthana, needed someone's help. But Himavant recovered faster than the two of them. "It's getting very dark, let's go towards that side," he said, and slowly began to step toward a small light visible somewhere in the distance. Debu no longer had the courage to walk with his arm around Prarthana’s shoulder. He tried to start some conversation but remained silent after seeing Himavant’s silence. He felt that in front of a strong personality like Himu, anyone would become weak; he wondered what would have happened to Prarthana if she had been alone this night. In fact, Himavant seemed like a man who had finished speaking; like someone walking towards the end of his life; like someone who had decided that his connection with the two of them and with this surrounding world was over. No, even if he had to be stubborn, he should have brought Urmila with him—Debu felt this a thousand times. If there was anyone who could handle Himu, it was her. But hadn't she said she wouldn't come at all? After sending Prarthana to Shivamogga, he had gone straight to Davangere and met Urmila first. She was sitting alone in the canteen drinking tea. She had said "congrats" in a cold tone for the Hyderabad engagement. But now, seeing that Prarthana has gone to Shivamogga to inform Himavant, he felt that he shouldn't have sent her alone.
