They seemed rooted to the spot. His mouth was half-open, and a couple of ants had already begun to crawl near his ear. Himavant placed his burning palm on Debu’s forehead. Debu’s eyes slowly turned toward him. Had he recognized him? Had he remembered something? With an indistinct, guttural sound, he fell silent, like one who had given up. Prarthana stood at a short distance. There was no one else in the room now. Himavant sat on the spread-out, pristine white sheet, facing northeast for a moment, as if composing himself, and then chanted the Pranava mantra in a clear voice. Prarthana lit the sesame oil lamp she had kept ready. After confirming that all the items he had requested were on the plate, Himavant began the second phase of the 'stambhana' (immobilization) process. Prarthana remained standing—Himavant, as if having forgotten that she was even there, went into a trance and continued chanting. It was a ritual lasting about an hour and three-quarters. He refilled the lamp with oil once in between, and at the end, he touched his finger to the soot of that same lamp and placed dots on the big toes of Debu, who lay motionless on the cot. With that, the ritual concluded. But when Himavant stood up, he had no strength left to walk. Prarthana ran over and offered support. His right arm was now on Prarthana’s shoulder. She led him, struggling with great effort, into the inner room of the bungalow. No one in the bungalow crossed their path. Even though there was a cot in the room, Prarthana spread a mat on the floor. Instead of using a pillow, she sat down herself and let Himavant rest his head on her lap. She unbuttoned his shirt, and with a cloth dipped in cool water, she began wiping his sweaty, warm chest with great affection. It was getting late into the night. Half-asleep from sheer exhaustion, Himavant looked at Prarthana once. Her eyes were a waterfall.

Outside the bungalow, thick darkness spread like sorrow. Everyone in the bungalow was in a deep, undisturbed sleep. For the first time, Debu lay peacefully, without cries, moans, or screams. His parents, too, had fallen into a sound sleep. Exhausted from the journey, Urmila also had her head on the pillow.
